Agrow

Overview

The Agro-food Productivity Nexus, under the management of Malaysia Productivity Corportation (MPC), is a development initiative that focuses on the productivity development in building connectivity of the agrofood supply chain.

The objective of this website is to enable easy, free, fast accessibility to the Malaysian agro-food information to increase the stakeholder competitiveness and productivity of their businesses. Include information, such as historic knowledge-based/records, current data/news, and future intelligence/predictions. Agrofood information includes the field of production, distribution, consumption, sustainability and environment and acts as a toolbox.

Responsibilities

For this project, I took a role as a UI UX designer and graphic designer. My responsibilities included: Visual Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing and Develop high fidelity mock-ups.

02 Define

The Problem

After listening and interviewing with a farmer, local, and students, we understand their pain points and troubles in this matter. From our understanding, our interviewees' common pain point is they feel like there is a missing gap of information in the digital world and a connectivity gap of the supply chain. This leads to miscommunication and misinformation. Making it harder to find a reliable source and accurate data.

03 Ideate

The Solution

Create an easy to use webpage and responsive mobile web solution to educate Malaysian about agriculture and help give a platform to grow their businesses from small farmer to bigger corporation. The access of the information should be a simple, easy and minimal effort for the user to research. Hence, visual clarity is taking into consideration to convey information effectively.

Design Decisions

01 Enabling user to interact with the UI

Several details are required for the users to fill in as standard protocol and guidelines to verify their details on the signup page. Some of the input filled is not necessary by a different group of users. The responsive enabling gradually enabling user interacts with certain user interface elements. Hence, this method can prevent error by restricting the available selections through different steps of the task.

02 Implementing tabs

Dealing with a multilevel panel and windows. As a designer, we want to allow the user to navigate various levels from one point to another on the UI. Each level contains a different set of content grouped to avoid potential confusion, and users can filter out their options and navigate straight to their desired option with ease.

03 Dividing the dataset into smaller groups

When dealing with a large set of ordered data that is difficult to display on one page, the user needs to view specific items. The solution I came out with is splitting the contents of a website into discrete pages. This method helps from overwhelming the user with a mass of data. On this page, we also give a little bit more control for the user to let users choose if they want to sort the search result by popularity, rating, recommendation, or alphabetical order.

05 Evaluation

Finding:

We pitched Agrow to a crowd and panel of stakeholders. We came as 3rd runner up. Overall, it's a great experience to be involved in.

The feedback I received after we conduct the user testing and feedback from stakeholder are:

    User's feedback.
  • The pages are easier and very pleasing to scan. However, the website doesn't contain enough information.
  • The registration forms are too complicated, with too many details. I don't have enough details right now, so I cannot sign up.
  • I like the ideas of the tab. We don't have to change to a different page. Hence, making it easier to get information.


    Stakeholders' feedback.
  • The website is clean and minimal, but I think a lot of missing information.
  • Why not use icons or pictures instead?
  • I like to see a more consistent look and feel
  • Why is the avatar is a girl in the suit?

Outcome

Although we came in 3rd place, I am happy we share my idea, learn, and improve my design thinking process. This project makes me realized there is more to just the web design and aesthetic webpage. I learned about the functionality and user interface, and I started exploring user experience even further.

Retrospective

A few things I have learned throughout this project is:

  • Organizing Content. In this project, I made the mistake of not planning how I want to organize my content. Hence, my design doesn't have a consistent look and feel.
  • Focus on the problem. At the end of the day, it is the user's pains that we will be solving for so keeping that front of mind is important as it's easy to lose sight of this when you're to focus on the result and the aesthetic.
  • Technology limitation. I started this project with research and understanding stakeholder point of view. We were introduced to the developer. However, we have a tough time understanding the developer's platform and her viewpoint. Ended up, she wanted to build a mobile web instead.
