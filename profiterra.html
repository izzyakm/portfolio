Profiterra

Overview

Profiterra is an online accounting and consulting platform that helps small business owners to grow into a profitable business by providing online bookkeeping, accounting services, and financial advice for small businesses in Malaysia.

This was a stepping stone for my career to dive deeper into UX context. I was able to practice my UX skills as well as sharpen my UI skills. I spent most of my time working closely with the founder and a small team to build Profiterra.

Responsibilities

For this project, I took a role as a UI UX designer and graphic designer. My responsibilities included: Visual Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing and Develop high fidelity mock-ups.

I worked alongside with a web developer to convert the prototype into a functional website.

9 September 2020 - 9 December 2020

We believe that when business owners are successful it can impact on the lives of people around them and building a successful society lifting others- Profiterra

Overview of my design thinking process.

01 Empathy

Interview

On 8 September 2020, I received a phone call from Farah (founder) to discuss her business. We further discuss her requirements through WhatsApp. We managed to have a quick face to face meeting on September 11. I prepared some of the business questionnaires to guide the interview session. We discussed her business plan and goals to understand their objectives and their brand identity.

Then I proceeded to understand her target user by conducting user research. During this research, because we are under the second wave of Covid19, I interviewed close family and friends who own small businesses through Whatsapp and video calls.

After listening, some of my findings are:

33% of small business owners unaware of services provided by an accountant.

66% of small business owners have trouble and get frustrated managing their business bookkeeping.

2/3 of small business owners feel hiring an accountant is expensive.

User Personas

Zafrul bin Shamsul is a local businessman. Zafrul and his brother started a supplier inspection company in 2019. They hire their own accountant to record their bookkeeping and tax, although they are still new and small. They do not know any accounting firm and have no knowledge of their services. Hence, they feel like all accounting firms are not for small business owners.

Adibah Binti Aziz owns a real estate and investment consulting company. She has been in the real estate industry for 6 years. In 2018, she decided to start her own real estate company focusing Japan market. She hasn't found any accounting firm on the internet to read about their services. She is very busy with her clients, making her no time to call or send an email to any accounting firm to understand their services.

Liza Binti Ahmad and her husband owns a food and beverages company. From night-market now they own a stall selling Nasi Ayam. Liza has a background in accounting. Hence she managed her own business accounting. She enjoyed doing it and has no problem doing it on her own. However, now she wants to grow her business. She needs experts to guide her. However, she feels like she didn't trust any accounting firm because she doesn't understand its business nature.

02 Define

The Problem

After listening and interviewing with our small business owners, we understand their pain points and troubles in this matter. From our understanding, our interviewees' common pain point is they feel like there is a missing gap of all accounting firms in their digital world presence. It's making it harder for small business owners to understand what services they provide and what options they have.

03 Ideate

The Solution

Our main goal to solve this problem is to create an informative desktop web page that allows Profiterra to share their accounting services and educate users on the importance of accounting tasks for their businesses. The solution includes simple and easy navigation for their potential client to trust their services and book for a free consultation.

Design Decisions

01 Fluid Navigation

To allow fluid navigation from one section to another, I implemented top-level navigation bars at the highest point in the user interface architecture, enabling the user to dive into a broad group of contents placed together based on some shared relationship. Include a sitemap in the footer of every webpage, so when your users reach the end of the contents, there is an obvious and instantly accessible navigation tool waiting to help them move freely around the website, or at the very least to jump to another level within the site hierarchy. To keep users' attention on the free consultation, redundant work is implemented by adding a contact form section.

02 Visual hierarchy

Halfway through working on the project, we realized that our client is so passionate to explain in great detail each of the services provided. This makes the webpage have some visual issues. Research showed users tend to scan the page before they stumbled upon something that caught their sight before reading the text. Here, we use graphics to catch people's attention and direct their sight to the bold and bigger heading text before moving to the actual content. This visual hierarchy makes users scan for information clearly and easily.

03 Resolve conflict by conduct user testing

Later on in this project, I had a small conflict with my client. he insisted that the contact page will have a detailed text instead of a bullet point of information. However, it would create a bad user experience for users to read text by text to find important information. After discussing the solution, I convinced my client to conduct user testing to understand and simulate eye-tracking studies.

Outcome

It was my first time working on the user experience. I get to explore most of the design process. However, I'm still in the learning process and still looking forward to conducting physical user testing. This project was an extremely steep learning curve. It was an eye-opening experience that taught me a lot about being organized and knowing when and where to focus your energy and efforts.

So far, I did my best to translate from what I have learned on UI design. It definitely has room for improvement in the near future.

Retrospective

A few things I have learned throughout this project is:

  • Don't worry too much about the detail. In this project, I made the mistake of worrying about the look of the UI. Upon reflecting, I need to reprioritize the UX.
  • Communication. I learned it's important to constantly communicate with my team to make sure we all on the same page and have the same goals.
  • Technology limitation. When working on the project, I don't understand how technology constraints can impact my design and vice versa. I learned that I need to understand the developer's technology consider that when designing the website.
